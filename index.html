Our mission

Truth matters. Protect it. Share it. — Say it loud.

What Doctors Wish You Knew is a free, ad-free search bar for health truth — the honest answer your doctor would give you if they had all the time in the world.

Every answer is reviewed by doctors and backed by credentialed sources. We sell nothing, we take no ads, and we never sell your data — see our Privacy Policy. When the science is clear, we say so plainly. When it isn’t, we say that too.

How it works

1

Search

Type any claim, supplement, medication, condition, test, or symptom.

2

See the evidence

A calm Strong / Mixed / Weak rating, what the studies actually show, and safety notes — no scare tactics.

3

Get the bottom line

One plain-English takeaway, what to do, and the words to raise it with your own doctor.

Who reviews the answers

Answers are reviewed by the WDWYK doctors and supported by named, credentialed experts — judged on expertise, never follower count. We don’t name individual reviewing physicians publicly: it protects our clinicians and keeps answers from going stale as credentials change. The science leads; the people stay in the background.

Our pledge

No ads. We’re powered by supporters who believe in honest health — and none of them get a say in what we tell you. We exist to inform people and protect those they love, not to sell anything. This is education, not medical advice; always talk to your own doctor.

Privacy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 3, 2026

The short version: we collect almost nothing. If you join our email list or ask us to cover a topic, we get your email address and any note you add — and we use it only to send you what you asked for. We don’t sell, rent, or trade your information to anyone, ever. No ads, no ad trackers.

What Doctors Wish You Knew (“WDWYK,” “we,” “us”) is in beta — we’re still building, so this policy may grow as we add features. We’ll keep it honest and current. This policy covers whatdoctorswishyouknew.com. It doesn’t cover other sites we link to, which have their own policies.

What we collect

Email you give us. If you join the list or use a “request a topic” form, we collect your email address and any optional message you type. No name required.

Anonymous site statistics. We use Cloudflare Web Analytics, which Cloudflare states does not use cookies or other client-side storage and does not collect or use visitors’ personal data to identify them. It tells us things like how many people viewed a page — not who they are.

Ordinary technical data. Like every website, our host and security provider, Cloudflare, processes basic technical information your browser sends (such as your IP address and browser type) to deliver the site and protect it from abuse. We don’t use this to build a profile of you.

How we use it

Only to send you the updates or answers you asked for, to see which topics to prioritise, to understand in aggregate which pages help, and to keep the site secure. We do not use your information for advertising, and we never sell or share it for cross-context behavioural advertising.

Who helps us run this

We keep this list short on purpose. Each company acts on our behalf under its own privacy terms:

MailerLite stores our email list and sends our emails. MailerLite states it “will never sell, rent, or lease your Personal Data,” stores data in the EU, and is ISO/IEC 27001 certified. See MailerLite’s privacy policy.
Cloudflare hosts the site, protects it, and provides our cookieless analytics. See Cloudflare’s privacy policy.

If we add a donation or payment tool later, we’ll name it here before turning it on.

Cookies and “Do Not Track”

We don’t use cookies to advertise to you or follow you around the web; our analytics are cookieless. If your browser sends a “Do Not Track” signal, there’s nothing for us to stop, because we don’t track you across other sites and don’t sell or share your data for behavioural advertising.

Your choices and rights

You can unsubscribe anytime — every email has a one-click link. You can also ask us to show, correct, or delete what we have, and we extend these choices to everyone, wherever you live. If you’re in California, you have rights under California law, and we don’t sell your information. To make any request, email privacy@whatdoctorswishyouknew.com.

For visitors in the EU/EEA & UK (GDPR)

Because many of our visitors are in Europe, we apply these protections to everyone’s data here.

Who’s responsible. The data controller is What Doctors Wish You Knew, a US-based service that’s open to the world — contact privacy@whatdoctorswishyouknew.com with any data question.

Why we use your data (lawful basis). We use your email to send what you asked for, on the basis of your consent — withdraw anytime via the unsubscribe link. We use cookieless, aggregate analytics and basic security on the basis of our legitimate interests in running and protecting the site.

Your rights. You can access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or port your data, and withdraw consent at any time. You can also complain to your data-protection authority — in the Netherlands, the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens. Email privacy@whatdoctorswishyouknew.com to exercise any of these.

Where your data is processed. Our email provider (MailerLite) stores data in the EU; some providers such as Cloudflare may process data in the US under recognised safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses or the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework).

Children

This is general health information for a broad audience, and parts of it are written for parents. It is not directed to children under 13, and we don’t knowingly collect their information. Our email list is for adults — you must be at least 16 to subscribe. If you believe a child gave us their information, email us and we’ll delete it.

How long we keep your email

We keep your email until you unsubscribe or ask us to delete it — then we remove it. We don’t keep what we don’t need.

Security & changes

We take reasonable steps to protect the little information we hold, and we keep our footprint small to keep your risk small. No service can promise perfect security. If we change this policy, we’ll post the new version here with a new date, and flag anything that really matters.

Important to know

Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: June 3, 2026

What Doctors Wish You Knew gives you honest, doctor-reviewed health information so you can understand the evidence and have a better conversation with your own doctor. It’s here to complement the care your doctor gives you — never to replace it. We can’t see you, examine you, or know your history, so nothing here is personal medical advice.

Information, not personal medical advice

Our answers are general health information based on published sources. They are not a diagnosis, a treatment plan, or advice for your particular situation, and reading them doesn’t create a doctor–patient relationship between you and WDWYK or anyone who reviews our content.

Your own doctor comes first

Only a professional who can examine you and knows your history can give advice that truly fits you. Always talk to your doctor, pharmacist, or another qualified clinician before you start, stop, or change any treatment, medicine, supplement, or test — and bring your questions. We even give you the words to raise a topic; the point is to use them with your own doctor, not instead of one.

In an emergency, don’t wait

If you think you may have a medical emergency, call your local emergency number (911 in the U.S.) or go to the nearest emergency room right now. Never delay getting care, and never ignore advice from your own clinician, because of something you read here. If you’re in the U.S. and thinking about harming yourself, or you’re in emotional crisis, call or text 988 — the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline.

How we keep answers honest

Every answer is reviewed by one of the WDWYK doctors before we publish it. But medicine changes quickly and no source is ever perfect, so we keep updating our answers as the evidence changes. If you’re a clinician or expert and you spot something that needs fixing or a closer look, please tell us — we’ll review it. Getting it right matters more to us than being right.

Your decisions are your own

We share information to help you decide with your doctor. To the fullest extent the law allows, this site and its content are provided “as is,” and What Doctors Wish You Knew is not liable for choices made based on this general information.
